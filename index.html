UX/UI Case Study: Health Companion Super App

A mobile app experience for managing medications, appointments, and communication with healthcare professionals.

Role: UX Research • UX Design • UI Design
Tools: Figma • Google Forms • Notion
Timeline: 2–3 weeks
Deliverables
  • Research summary + personas
  • User journey map
  • Wireframes + iterations
  • Clickable prototype
  • Usability testing plan + findings
Outcome

A clear end-to-end flow for reminders, appointment scheduling, and patient-provider messaging.

Problem

People with chronic conditions often juggle multiple medications and appointments. Missed doses and forgotten follow-ups can lead to worse outcomes and stress.

Proposed Solution

A health companion “super app” that supports reminders, tracking, appointment management, and communication.

Key Features

  • Medication reminders + adherence log
  • Doctor appointment scheduling + calendar
  • Messaging / sharing updates with professionals

Design Principles

  • Clarity over density
  • Accessibility (type scale, contrast, tap targets)
  • Reduce cognitive load

User Research

Describe your research methods and what you learned (surveys, interviews, secondary research).

Methods

  • Survey (n=__): adherence habits, reminder preferences, pain points
  • Interviews (n=__): routines, barriers, motivations
  • Secondary research: chronic-care UX patterns and accessibility best practices

Key Insights

✅ Users want fewer steps to confirm a dose taken.
✅ Appointments need one-screen clarity (date/time/location/prep).
✅ Messaging must feel safe and simple (no clutter).

Usability Testing

Plan

  • Goals: validate reminder flow, appointment scheduling clarity, messaging discoverability
  • Participants: __ users, mix of chronic condition experience + general users
  • Tasks: log a medication, schedule an appointment, send a message
  • Data: task success rate, time on task, confusion points, post-task rating

Key Findings

⚠️ Users hesitated when confirming “Taken” vs “Skipped”.
✅ Appointment card layout improved scannability.
⚠️ “Message provider” label needed stronger affordance.

Iterations after testing

  • Renamed actions: “Taken” / “Not taken” with a short tooltip
  • Added primary CTA styling to messaging entry point
